Statement of Intent

Carrying on from my previous work of representing the fertility rate of New Zealand. This project is to represent some of the underlining factors as to why the fertility rate of New Zealand is where it is at now as well as explorer where it is heading and where it has come from. I will iterate over different designs to find an optimal way to make this as clear and engaging as possible.
I will also analyse some fo the factors and see how much they impact the fertility rate of a country and using the Demographic Transition Model (DTM), I will predict the vague future of how New Zealand's fertility rate is expected to change.
I will also reflect on my work and how I can improve it if I had more time and reflecting on the skills I have developed during this process of designing.



Fertility Rate

Fertility rate is the average number of children born per woman. (assuming she were to live until the end of her reproductive life). Therefore if the fertility rate of a region is less than (or equal to) 2 births per woman (BPW), it is expected the population will shirk as there are more deaths than births. If the fertility rate is much greater than 2 (BPW) the population is expected to grow. And if the fertility rate is slightly more than 2 (BPW), the population is expected to stay approximately the same (due to premature deaths).
As discussed in a previous blog post New Zealand's fertility rate has been dropping. In 1920 the fertility rate was 3.26 BPW. This fell to 1.61 BPW in 2020.
Fertility rate is an important indicator for a country to compare it's self with others and determine (broadly) if it has good health.


Fertility Factors

The fertility rate is influenced by many factors. Such as education, health care, quality of life and so on.

Education

Education is a very important aspect of fertility rate, as education improves the population becomes more aware the risks and responsibilities of having children.

source: https://ourworldindata.org/fertility-rate

The graph above from ourworldindata.org [1] shows a strong inverse corelation between the number of years of education and the fertility rate. As sex education improves more people understand the reasons to use contraceptives, this makes education one of the largest factors influencing contraceptive prevalence.
Contraceptive has a fairly strong trend with fertility rate as shown the the graph below.

source: https://ourworldindata.org/fertility-rate

An interesting study was done in Tanzania in 2013 by the US NHS [2]. In this study, the researchers found that there was wide spread misinformation about the use of condoms. One of the findings were that nearly half of the interview participants agreed with the statement "Small worms in new, unopened condoms cause HIV." [2] This shown the the table below. This is one example as to misinformation about contraceptives which hinder the use of condoms.

source: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3432708/

Labour Force

There is also a trend between the percent of women in the work force and the fertility rate. As more women enter the work force they have less time to spend working children and thus child birth drops in priority. And as work is a monetary source, while children are a monetary sink, some countries such as Japan have had to encourage more women to give birth with cash rewards [3].
This trend is not nearly as well defined as some of the other factors which could be for a few reasons, such as it being less of a factor or because it is not a factor at all, but instead other factors are influencing fertility rate and labour force participation.

source: https://ourworldindata.org/fertility-rate

Health Care

Likely the biggest factor influencing fertility rate is health care. As health care improves children are more likely to survive longer and grow up. This then mean that women don't need to have multiple children to have a good chance of one of them living. And since most of them would live if they had good health care, if a woman still had many kids she would have to take care of them. As a result the fertility decreases as health care improves.
As health care isn't quantifiable by it self the graph below is of child mortality rate, which is one measure of the quality of health care.

source: https://ourworldindata.org/fertility-rate

Quality of Life

lastly another large factor influencing fertility rate is quality of life. As quality of life improves, the fertility rate decreases as a result. This is because the quality of life is a direct result of all the other factors combined, better health care, better education, better equality. As discussed in the previous paragraphs these all influence the fertility rate in different ways.
The graph below is of the fertility rate over the human development index (HDI). the HDI is a measure of the quality of life of a country. It is calculated using the life expectancy, average number of years in education and GDP per capita (adjusted for living cost) of a country. This is meant to give a good overview as to how a country is doing overall rolling many factors into one measure.

source: https://ourworldindata.org/fertility-rate


Other factors

Since fertility rate is a very broad indicator, there are many other factors to consider. For example, China had a very low fertility rate, due to the One-child-policy. It also doesn't take skewed demographics.
Despite having many edge cases, fertility rate useful as a metric to broadly look at a counties health and wellbeing.


The Future of Fertility

The demographic transition model[4], is a very basic predictive model of how a countries birth rates and death rates change over time. The model is split up into 5 different stages which start with a high birth and death rate and then drop down at separate times to a low birth and death rate. This is explained in the section above about healthcare. New Zealand is currently in stage 4 of the DTM and is likely to progress to stage 5 soon.
During the first 3 stages of the DTM, the fertility rate is greater than 2 leading to an increasing population. During stage 4 the fertility rate is less than 2 leading to a decreasing population. And during stage 5 the fertility rate settles around 2 leading to a stable population. Of course a population can never be entirely stable but the DTM is a rough approximation.


Sketches

I created a range of sketches to explore the different factors and find different methods to represent them. These sketches were very low fidelity but gave a good idea to different approaches I would be able to take. I reached out to some peers, lecturers and friends to provide feedback on my ideas which the gladly gave. As shown below (each post it note is a different piece of feedback)

From this I decided the best idea to move forward with would be lady pushing prams past and going into a range hospitals, each looking more sophisticate, representing the link to healthcare. So I iterated. From the feedback I received it seemed I should simplify the idea more. So I changed it from 5 hospitals to 3 hospitals. I also removed the hospitals and instead showed the advancement of quality of life over time.
This way I would be able to show quality of life as a broad factor rather than one specific factor.

I started by consulting an expert in pixel art as I am not an artist by any means and I needed some advice. Hes advice was very useful so I decided to trial it on creating a terraria character. This allowed me to learn about different techniques and how to use them to create pixel art. From this I started creating some assets using an online pixel art editor. Unfortunately it seemed that most of my feedback was that it was difficult to see how the art would link together in a loop. So I wasn't able to get much useful feedback on purely the assets. So I created a looped animation in processing.

This iteration was good but didn't have any background images which made it look kind of bland. So I added some background images. I also changed the first lady to having 8 prams to having 4 along side this I tested different frame rates to find which was best.

After positive feedback I made the background assets more detailed. The past background became based on an early victorian/late industrial revolution factory setting. The modern day became based on the Auckland city skyline, based off a collection of reference images. So some of the larger building are depicted such as the Skytower, the Lumley Building and the Viro Building (along side others).
I made the assets of the ladies not outlined to fit with the design theme, This meant I had to change how I represented boundaries on elements such as their arms, for this instead of outlines I used shadows and slightly different shades.
I also had to optimise my code since processing is a notoriously slow language which meant that the speed of the animation was slow and inconsistent depending on how much it had to render.

In my 4th iteration I decided to instead of showing the past and future generally, to pick a specific time frame (in this case 100 years), luckily my animations were relativity similar to styles representing 100 years ago and 100 years in the future, I just needed to adjust the number of prams to match the relevant dates. I also decided to fill in the bottom parts fo the screen to make it more exciting, to do this I made the grass field into a garden with a picket fence. I also added a building into the future panel as some feedback one the previous iteration suggested the third panel seemed a bit bland.

The feedback I received from the previous iteration was that it was the background was too distracting from the prams and it might be better to compose the image so the lady is walking on the bottom third rather than roughly in the middle. It was also suggested that I create a clearer distinction between the present and future skies since they skies were very similar colours.
To fix the visual hierarchy issue I increased the size of the sprites substantially. I shifted the whole image down which also reduced the amount of unused space for the fields, and made the modern sky a bit mirky firstly as a nod to air pollution lowering the quality of life and secondly to create a clear distinction between the present and future.
for performance reasons I also switched having 3 separate background image files which can easily be swapped out individually to having one background total. This does mean I need to draw more pixels each draw cycle but reduces the amount of time importing the files.

The feedback on this iteration was that it was clear to see that the technology of the times were influence the number of children women. Which was my final goal. I also received positive feedback about the aesthetics. One thing which was suggested but due to performance and time constraints I didn't implement was creating a parallax effect by moving some foreground assets slowly to the left, and the background assets to the right even slower. Although this would have looked very good, it would also distract form the lady with the prams, thus diluting my message.

Throughout this project I have used a lot of different techniques to compose my designs and through many iterations ended up with a final concept that I was happy with.
