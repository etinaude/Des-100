Prototyping

Logo prototypes

Some logo designs I prototyped

Prototyping is a great way to create basic ideas to follow and do divergent ideation. Through prototyping you can find ideas which you like and filter the ones you don't this allows you to get a really good final product in a relatively short amount of time.
I'll focus on a case study of a logo design I created for a software development and design start up. The images on the right show the first stage of prototypes which were then refined. I felt the middle two rows were the best logos for a range of reasons, so I next curated and deleted the logos which didn't follow the aesthetic I was looking for.

final logo designs

This allowed me to not waste any time on continuing down paths of the logos I didn't like. This is one reason why prototyping is very valuable especially at the beginning of the design process. By doing this I was also able to speed up the design and asset creation process. Unlike most prototyping I was luckily able to land on a final main design from the first round of development. So I used the design and created a suit of assets to be used in different situations.



Prototyping in general

When I worked on my previous submissions I always started by prototyping, but They could have all been improved by creating more iterations of prototypes, this would have allowed me to create more refined ideas and better overall final submissions. Additionally it would allow me to explore more options and therefor learn about a wider range of ideas.

Prototyping is core to almost all innovative processes. It is nearly impossible to create a perfect product the first time you try so instead we prototype something which we know wont be the final product but will test the broad idea, then we can refine this again and again until we have the final product.

There are many ways in which prototyping can work, for design it might be creating a low fidelity prototype which doesn't look as good as the final product, for programming it might be creating a paper prototype or mvp which performs all the essential tasks with none of the bells, whistles or designs. For research it might be creating a theory which broadly works with many unaddressed edge cases.

None of these are final products which are going to be released but all allow us to work towards our goals.
