Micheal Crichton

Someone I admire

Micheal Crichton next to his best selling books
Credit: Ron Galella, Ltd./Ron Galella Collection via Getty Images

I admire Micheal Crichton. Crichton was an author most famous for writing Jurassic Park, but Crichton was much more than an author; he studied as a doctor at Harvard Medical school, produced and wrote the TV show ER, which won 116 awards, and he programmed computer games. Crichton is the only person to be the creator of the top book, movie and TV show in a single year… which he did twice… consecutively. As such, he has not only mastered a single craft but many. Crichton also mastered many technical disciplines; he lectured anthropology at Cambridge University, pioneered visual effects (winning him an Oscar) as well as programmed two computer games and movie budging software.

I admire him because he was able to span many skill sets and bridge the gap between the creative and the technical. He showed extraordinary skills in bringing together science with creativity in many of his novels which tended to be techno-science fiction; in these, he was able to both create an engaging narrative and write about complex scientific topics.


Bridging Gaps

Bridging the technical-design gap is something I aspire to do as both a designer and scientist. Although my medium will be different, I think this is an important skill that can be transposed across all media. Additionally, I would like to develop my writing and communication skills. Although this is not a branch of design, it is very important for any designer who needs to work with a client or team.


Crichton’s approach to his writing was to start by talking to as many experts in relevant fields, then use the collective knowledge of those experts to create a realistic novel, and he went as far as too add footnotes to scientific papers in some of his fiction novels, and a multi-page reference section. I think this approach is an excellent idea as there are always experts in a field regardless of how niche it is, and the vast majority of the time, they would want to help people learn about their niche. Most projects will have elements of cross-disciplinarity. Therefore I would like to adopt this style in order to better understanding of the other disciplines in work.
