Primitives

The Haiku

Final poster

This is a poster using primitive geometric designs to abstract the haiku by anonymous:

haikus are really dumb
seriously, the worst poems
they have no meaning

This haiku has a few themes running through it firstly is the irony, the poet could have chosen any type of poetry but they chose a haiku and wrote about a lack of meaning which in itself has multiple meanings, secondly there is an element of irritation as they are insulting an art form. Thirdly there is an air of acceptance as the word "poems" indicates that the poet does acknowledge that while they not appreciate haikus, they understand that others do. This acceptance is also shown by the fact that this is written into the form of a haiku .


Design Considerations

digital prototype

I used a digital tool to prototype and develop ideas which could be used in the final poster. Through this I went through many iterations and settled on this design. I used the white circle to emphasise the inside triangles more than the outside and create a balance between the outside and inside triangles since they are the same size and there are the same amount of them. The design has lots of symmetry, there are three axes of symmetry.

The Solution


Shapes

The design consists of 3 large triangles implied by the groupings of 2 small white triangles and one small black triangle, I've shown this in red. Then there is one central triangle outlined by three small black triangles, (highlighted in green), and lastly there are the negative space triangles shown in purple. This totals to 7 large triangles and 12 small triangles. The rule of odds additionally is applied through the different triangle.

Next the circles. There are the three orbiting green circles which __________. The main white circle which is used to put the center at the top of the visual hierarchy, and lastly the central circle shown in yellow. This central one forms a "radioactive" symbol with the triangles.


Final Solution

The nested shapes are a good representation of how meta the poem is in talking about itself, the irritation is shown by all the sharp abrupt points in the triangles, this is contrasted by the acceptance of the circles. As triangles consist of as few sides as possible to make a polygon and circles consist of the most, this creates a contrast, which I've utilised to show the different themes of the poem.

Lastly the analyse of a haiku which says that haikus have no meaning is irony about irony, which both follows the irony nature and the meta nature of the haiku.
