Primitives

The Haiku

Final poster

This is a poster using primitive geometric designs to abstract the haiku by an anonymous poet:

haikus are really dumb
seriously, the worst poems
they have no meaning

This haiku has a few themes running through it first is the irony. The poet could have chosen any type of poetry, but they chose a haiku. Second, there is an element of irritation as they are insulting an art form. Third there is an air of acceptance as the word "poems" indicates that the poet does acknowledge that while they do not appreciate haikus, they understand that others do. This acceptance is also shown by the fact that this is written in the form of a haiku.


Design Considerations

digital prototype

I used a digital tool to prototype and develop ideas that could be used in the final poster. Through this, I went through many iterations and settled on this design. I used the white circle to emphasise the inside triangles over the outside ones and create a balance between the outside and inside triangles since they are the same size and there are the same amount. The design has lots of symmetry, there are three axes of symmetry.

Shapes


different shapes used in the design

The design consists of 3 large triangles implied by the groupings of 2 small white triangles and one small black triangle; I've shown this in red. Then there is one central triangle outlined by three small black triangles (highlighted in green), and lastly, there are the negative space triangles shown in purple. This totals to 7 large triangles and 12 small triangles. The triangles form a repetition and circular pattern. The repetition is furthered by the use of only two shapes.

Next, the circles. There are three orbiting green circles which break the large triangles into smaller ones and aid in the repetition. The main white circle is used to put the centre at the top of the visual hierarchy, and lastly, the central circle shown in yellow. This central one forms a "radioactive" symbol with the triangles.


The nested shapes are a good representation of how meta the poem is in talking about itself, the irritation is shown by all the sharp, abrupt points in the triangles, this is contrasted by the acceptance of the circles. As triangles consist of as few sides as possible to make a polygon and circles consist of the most, this creates a contrast, which I've utilised to show the different themes of the poem.

Lastly, the analyse of a haiku and creating the poster about the meaning of a haiku which says that haikus have no meaning, is irony about the irony, which both follows the irony nature and the meta nature of the haiku.
