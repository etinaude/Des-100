Story Board

Final Poster

read top down



The basic plot of the story is that there is a student in an English class who gets bored while learning about poems so, strongly dislikes them. But then, in his own time, he discovers some great poems and develops an understanding as to why poems are great. He then attempts to write poems for years and years, never writing anything good and sinking into a deep depression, having an existential crisis.
One day, when he's getting old, he has a great plan for a poem, writes down the incredible poem and gets awarded the Nobel literature prize. While getting the award, he realises his life has not been a failure.


Aging

The main character ages throughout the storyboard with his hair growing out, as he forgoes shaving during the failing writing stage. Then growing shorter as the hair loss kicks in. Additionally, his hair grows lighter as the story progresses. This is used to show the time scale of the plot, ensuring that it is taken over many decades.


Colour

The image is predominantly in greyscale except for a few things similar to "Schindler's List" (Spielberg, 1993). I used hints of colour to highlight some important elements putting them at the top of the visual hierarchy. These elements are the book, lightbulb and the medal as they are life-changing moments for the main character. The colour also contrasts the greyscale of the rest of the storyboard to make the rest of it look sad.


Camera Angles

The establishing shot is a wide-angle used to set the scene of the classroom by using a wide-angle rather than an extreme wide-angle this makes the scale of the scene personal as it only focuses on the characters in the room. The camera then focuses on the main character using a medium shot showing "how they are interacting with the environment" (Filippo, 2019). The over the shoulder shot in panel three allows the viewer to see what's on the page, and the complimenting medium close up on the character's reaction shows the facial expressions as detailed as possible.

The next three panels show an over the shoulder shot of the character writing with more and more scraps of paper building up. This puts the viewer into a similar position as the main character, which makes the scene feel more relatable. The binge-drinking scene features a close-up shot to emphasise what the character is feeling.

Panel eleven features an extreme wide shot this is used to show the scale of the movie has changed now that the main character has affected more than the people in the classroom.
