Derailed

Final poster

Background

A movie poster design for the fictional movie "derailed". The movie is based around a train derailing and the ensuing tragedy on a mundane evening due to the driver becoming complacent and starts to drift off to sleep since everything looks normal. The image used is of a train and surrounding tracks in Parnell, Auckland. I used a long exposure technique to create a streak as a train passed by. I then used the digital drawing platform "krita" to edit and overlay text.

Layout

Rule of thirds

The basic layout of the poster has the street light on the top-left third corner the bottom third line is directly above the steel beam and the pillar for that beam is on the right third. All these combine to give good framing to the piece. Additionally as these solidify the rule of thirds I have placed the staring actors names into the thirds; centered in each sector.

Lighting and Colour

The street light is as the top of the visual hierarchy because it is contrasted well with all surrounding being the brightest part of the image entirely encapsulated in the darkest part of the image. The long exposure of the photo matched with the mild "blurry" effect shows what the scene would look like through someone eye who were sleepy or drunk, as the driver in the movie would be.

The poster uses a complementary colour scheme between the dark blue and the brown/orange. This has been used to create a greater contrast. The brown is used to create a boring or "dull" (Chapman, 2010) aesthetic to further the idea of everything looking normal.

Fonts

For the credits and starring actors names I picked the font "Univers LT Std 39 Thin Ultra Condensed" (Adrian Frutiger) firstly because it is a thin font which allows me to add more information on the page without taking up too much space, secondly it's a reasonably easy font to read even when small and lastly it's an industry standard font for movie poster credits.

For the title and date I used the font "Inter" as it is a large bold and easy to read font. Its characters are also wide placing it higher up this visual hierarchy.
