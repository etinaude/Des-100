Derailed

Final poster

Background

A movie poster design for the fictional movie "derailed". The movie is based around a train derailing and the ensuing tragedy on a mundane evening due to the driver becoming complacent since everything looks normal.






Layout

Rule of thirds

The basic layout of the poster has the street light on the top-left third corner the bottom third line is directly above the steel beam and the pillar for that beam is on the right third. All these combine to give good framing to the piece. Additionally as these solidify the thirds grid I have placed the staring actors names into the thirds; centered in each sector.


Fonts

I created a few sketches of ideas to solve this issue and settled on a design which would clip on to the top of the printer and surround the threaded rod; I thought it would be easiest to continue ideating and sketching my designs in 3d so I could quickly refine it and have a visual representation of my model. Unfortunately, while designing this, I realised that it would take unnecessary filament and time to print, and it's always a good idea to refine and improve designs.


I then refined this idea by reducing the width by 50%, and instead of having a solid block of wasted filament, I created a raised holder for the rod. I was happy with this design's look but again realised that I could reduce the filament used by a considerable margin. It didn't need to fully clip on to the printer since it doesn't need to be very secure; after all, no force would be applied to it. This means I have a small clip slightly larger than the rod. My third prototype was finally small enough to be entirely happy with. But it shared another flaw with all the rest of the designs so far: they might increase friction on the rod. In the best-case scenario, it uses some extra electricity to drive the motors, and in the worst-case, it could ruin a long print.


The Solution


My final design was a small-cap that would fit on the top of the rod. This was very small, which means it used minimal filament and time (taking only 5 minutes to print). Additionally, it reduces the likelihood of interfering with the printhead. This design would also only touching the rod, which means there would be no extra friction. The rounded edges at the bottom both help make it look slightly better and make it substantially easier to insert the rod. The top was not rounded as that could help snag the cables between it and the top metal plate.
