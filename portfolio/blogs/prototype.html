Modification

The problem

Cabling caught behind threaded rod

We have two Anycubic i3 Mega 3d printers at home, which are good entry level printers for an affordable price. Unfortunately since the price is so low it come with a few fatal flaws. One of these flaws occurs when printing very large objects due to the way cabling is bent there is a high chance of it being caught behind a threaded pole. This means that when the print head needs to move away from the pole it can't since its stuck. Often this means that the print fails, which can be very bad for a 40 hour print using $60 of filament, Therefor my project was to mitigate this point of failure before anyone used the printer for another big project.


Cabling caught behind threaded rod

I created a few sketches of ideas to solve this issue, and settled on a design which would clip on to the top of the printer and go around the threaded rod, I thought it would be easiest to continue my design in 3d so I could quickly refine it and have a visual representation of my model. Unfortunately, while designing this I realised that it would take unnecessary filament and time which wouldn't matter much as the model was already very small, but it's always a good idea to refine and improve designs.
