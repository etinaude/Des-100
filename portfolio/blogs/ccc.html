Laser Cutting

The Problem

Tangled Headphones

My headphones are always getting tangled into knots when I put them in my bag. Because of this, my headphones break reasonably often, so I go through more pairs of headphones than I should; this is firstly bad for the environment, secondly, expensive. I needed a solution to keep my headphone untangled and easy to unwrap.

I think I can create a small holder out of laser cut MDF within the requirements of the project to solve this!


The Prototypes

First Prototype

I started by creating prototypes using cardboard to get the basic form of the holder correct. I decided on a square shape with an indent on two opposite sides to keep the cable from slipping off. I changed and expanded this indent until I felt it would comfortably hold my headphones. The Issue with this design was the headphones had a tendance to unravel themselves from the holder since there was no mechanism for keeping the ends down.


Second Prototype

My second prototype included slots for keeping the two ends of the cable down which seemed to work quite well. I tried a few different sizes and shapes of slots and found that tapered ends worked best since they could grip either the smaller wires or bigger wire. This has the added benefit of if I get new headphones with a different size cable, the holder would still work.


The Solution


Final design

Armed with my prototypes and the information I got while testing, I created an SVG file using Inkscape. As I was using a more precise tool, I would be able to produce finer edits and nicer curves to make my design look much better than the prototypes. I taped the large indents and curved their edges to make it look better (and mimic the shape of a jigsaw puzzle piece). I also added four slots I could use to hold the ends of the cables. Now if I rolled my headphones a bit looser or tighter than normal, I can then use a different slot and not have to waste time re-rolling them.


Cut Solution

As you can see, the headphones are nicely rolled up and won't come undone in my bag.

Overall this was a successful project, although I do think there are many areas for improvement. In another iteration, I might use a clip to hold down the ends rather than an indent; this clip would be useful since it would be stronger at holding the ends down and could be used to clip onto the inside of my bag, so I don't need to fumble around the bottom to try to find them every time I want to use my headphones.
