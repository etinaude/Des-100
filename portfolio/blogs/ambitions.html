My Design Ambitions

My Design History and Interests

I have a few different design interests in which I have history in, and could pursue. I worked as a product designer at Shwoop where I worked with Tiger Guo Design Studio to create a "Shwoop Boop" - an interactive display to increase engagement for the platform.
I am also a creative technologist at Unleash Space where I help member prototype ideas and advise on designs.
But my main design interest is user-experience design. Currently I am employed as a research assistant working with the habits team in the Faulty of Medical Heath Sciences, where I design the user interface for the chatbot app.
Lastly, I actively work as a freelance UX designer and general design consultant. I create websites and web apps as well as provide advice as to how to improve designs, including:

  • Asset and logo design
  • Circuit and hardware design
  • UI/UX design
  • Product design

Etinaude Dev

Etinaude dev logo

Since I have such a wide range of design interests, I have a few different options as to what I could focus on. One of my current paths is to transform my freelancing work into a startup business and start employing some UI designers and front end developers to help me complete projects quicker. I have started to work towards this goal, and hopefully, in the next few months, I will employ my first staff member. In 10 years, I could hopefully be running a successful design consultancy studio focusing on helping emerging startups through this plan.

Research (and Development)

Another possible route I am looking into is becoming a researcher in creative technologies. I'd be able to design machines to help designers and research different design techniques and technological advancements of these machines. Alternatively, becoming a full-stack programmer where I would create intuitive interfaces that work seamlessly with the back-end code.

Assumptions

I will focus on the Etinaude Dev path for this section
  1. I don't switch fields of interest
  2. I continue to get a steady flow of work
  3. Clients who pay a decent wage
  4. I will have enough time to work on this outside of work and uni
  5. I make enough connections to provide long term business
  6. A large client decides to join
  7. Good employees join
  8. I get lucky
  9. Velocity, the Icehouse and other incubators decide to support me
  10. I keep developing my design skills

Testing my assumptions should be quite straightforward. For the first one, if I get good work opportunities or develop a strong interest in a different field, I will re-evaluate my goals and work towards a new one. My current goals will adapt and change; they are by no means steadfast. If I cannot find enough clients or clients who pay enough, then my assumption that I have a good business model is incorrect.
