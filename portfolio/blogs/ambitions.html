Reverse Engineering

3rd angle drawing

Measurements

For the model which I reverse engineered I chose my studying desk. This ended up being a good decision since I didn't need to move away from my workstation to measure anything.
As the desk had a wide range of dimensions I used a measuring tape for larger dimensions and a digital calliper for finer measurements, I chose a digital calliper to minimize the read errors of a vernier scale and to speed up measuring.
I wrote down the measurements onto a physical sketch as I took them which I could easily refer back to when modelling, I didn't include the draw handles in this as they were too complicated to draw and keep accurate records of so instead I unscrewed one and measured it whilst I was modelling.

Modeling

Model

I created the model in Fusion 360 as it deals well with less organic shapes and is reasonably quick to model in. Nearly all (with the exception of the handles and gaps between draws) objects were created by extruding a single sketch on the top plane of the table. All the edges were then filleted as there is no such thing as a 90-degree angle in the real world. I tried to reflect this by filleting each edge to different degrees in relation to the physical desk.

Rendering


The Fusion 360 material library has a wide range of material textures to choose from so I was able to adapt a bamboo plywood texture by stretching and orientating the texture correctly turning it into a fairly similar texture to the physical desk, I then added colour matches the light blue paint as closely as possible leaving me with a very realistic render of the desk.
