Define Phase

Background

Brainstormed Ideas

Our idea of creating a better enrolment system for University of Auckland students has received positive feedback from user interviews and from surveys which means it was a good problem to move forward with a specific way we could tackle this project. To solidify our plan, we created a 6-square problem space to analyse the feedback we received. The six sections we used were - "patterns", "we met", "surprises", "interested to learn", "tensions" and "it would make a big difference". We found that the "surprises" section and the "interested to learn" section had a huge overlap as they both refer to new information, which we did not expect. We initially created our brainstorm on paper as a group during the class session but then for the sack of readability and to make it easier to share - we re-created it on miro.
Once we had defined our problem space using the brainstorm, our group decided to create a mind map and an AEIUO chart, which would help us derive a good problem statement.


AEIOU

AEIOU

Ayeon and I worked to create the AEIOU board on miro. The main activity is enrolling and finding more information about enrolment. This enrolment is mainly done by the students at their own homes while interacting with the website through their computer. The other activities and interactions would be to support that main activity. This would either be directly (for instance, a sub-activity) or, more indirectly, a background activity enabling the enrollment.


The Mind Map

Pretty Mind Map

I focussed on creating a mind map using Coggle. The four sections which I focussed on were:

  • the flaws of the current design using Nielsen's usability heuristics
  • the web pages which we would need to create
  • the students' workflow and enrolment path
  • the broad issues which repeatedly mentioned in interviews and our survey

We found that students felt that there was lots of information that they thought was missing or would be good to have on the page, so this is listed in the "pages" section. Once we had created the mind map, we found that our specific focus area would be around usability and user experience, so we needed our problem statement to reflect this. We decided to stick with the problem statement which we derived last week - creating a better enrolment experience for students.


How Might We...

We also Ideated to create a "how might we" HMW statement which would give a short problem statement and outline our area of focus. We iterated through a few different HMW statements from a few different perspectives, for example, "Boost enrolment rates by streamlining the enrolment experience." This HMW statement is from the University's perspective, which is aimed at money and higher enrolment rates. As a group, we felt that our focus was more on empowering the students to create more informed decisions.
Because of this, we merged and adapted a few of our HMW statements to form one which more accurately represented our goal and shifted to be from the students perspective. our final HMW statement is:

how might we Create a better enrolment experience for students by providing them more information about the courses and degree they’re enrolling in.

This perspective heavily focuses on the students' experience of enrolment and highlights we aim to provide more information to improve it.
